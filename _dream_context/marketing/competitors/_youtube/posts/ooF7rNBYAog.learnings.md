## 1. The four-pillar psychology spine (would feed → `playbooks/copy-formulas.md`)

Jared's claim: every good ad satisfies four checks. Verbatim:

> "the psychology really boils down to these four things." — `[01:25]`
> "Did we get their attention? Yes. Are we sparking curiosity? Yes. Are we hitting an emotional pain point? Yes. And are we connecting with our ICP? Yes." — `[04:34]`

| Pillar | Verbatim definition | Quote ref |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Attention | "you have about .5 seconds to actually grab someone's attention on the new internet" *(explicitly rejects the 3-second consensus)* | `[01:39]` |
| Curiosity | "all you really need to do with the ad is spark enough curiosity to get them to click" | `[02:19]` |
| Emotion | "Are you making them fearful? Are you making them excited? What core emotion are you connecting with" | `[02:42]` |
| Connection (to ICP) | "You need to connect with your [I]CP or the customer that you're trying to sell to" | `[03:15]` |

**Why it might generalise:** the 4-line recap at the end of the video restates the same shape — *"speaks directly to the ICP. It creates a curiosity gap, triggers an emotional response, and provides a clear path to a solution. And this is what makes the viewer feel like this is made for me."* `[13:33]`

## 2. Funnel-type → ad-job mapping (would feed → `playbooks/funnel-structures.md`)

Jared splits ads by what the funnel below them is doing:

- **Quiz / consumption funnel below** → ad does only curiosity + emotion. *"there's not a whole lot of selling done in these ads, but we're really pulling on their curiosity and on their emotion"* `[03:43]`
- **Long prospecting VSL** → ad does the full sale. *"you take a cold scroller completely to a buyer in the ad. That is an example of where you're basically selling all in that original ad"* `[02:02]`
- **Listicle / quiz / strong landing page** → ad only needs curiosity. *"the bottom of your funnel, whether it be… a listical, a quizfunnel, or just a really good landing page… all you need is curiosity in some ads"* `[02:23]`

**Implication:** brief writers must declare the downstream funnel before they draft copy. Same product, different funnel = different ad job.

## 3. Working ad examples (would feed → `playbooks/hooks.md` + `playbooks/copy-formulas.md`)

Four real ads Jared's agency shipped, each tagged with the pillars it leans on. **Quote the headlines verbatim — these are the hook examples; do not paraphrase.**

### 3a. Fertility / quiz funnel — *dark + curiosity + female ICP*
- **Headline (verbatim):** *"Stop blaming yourself for not seeing that positive [test]. Take the quiz and see what's stopping you."* `[04:13]`
- ICP read: *"selling to women in a very emotional time. They're probably thinking, 'What's wrong with me?'"* `[04:03]`
- Funnel: quiz funnel.
- Pillars: attention ✓ curiosity ✓ emotional pain ✓ ICP-connect ✓

### 3b. Looksmaxing tool — *dark + curiosity + Gen-Z male ICP, doom-scroll context*
- **Headline (verbatim):** *"What if one tool gave you everything you need to ascend?"* `[05:32]`
- Visual frame (per Jared): *"young person maybe 16 to 22 doom scrolling at night. It's dark and there's this face looking back at them"* `[05:14]`
- Niche-jargon ICP signal: *"ascend is like a very specific term in the… looks maxing space. So that's why we use that term because it's giving us that connection with our ICP"* `[05:39]`

### 3c. Golf foursome meme — *humour + connection + Gen-Z/millennial male ICP*
- **Headline (verbatim):** *"The foresome experience you dream about."* `[06:53]`
- ICP read: *"we're not selling to the old guard of golfers… the Gen Z, the millennial golfer who's going to put down 12 Miller lights on… 18 holes on a weekend with his boys"* `[07:00]`
- Secondary lever — share-loop as AOV multiplier: *"we're also increasing the average order value by making an ad like this because this is an ad that they would share to their friends"* `[07:26]`

### 3d. Dip-cup cleaner — *humour + connection + male ICP, partner-friction angle*
- **Headline (verbatim):** *"Made for spitters, not quitters."* `[07:34]`
- Mechanism: punchline → share with friends → indirect product-pain match (his girl complains about the dip cup).

## 4. Anti-patterns (would feed → `playbooks/anti-patterns.md`)

Direct rejections from the transcript:

- **Ingredient-list / spec-sheet ads.**
  > "These are not just products with… the ingredients that the product has. Nobody cares. News flash. These are not just… product pictures with… how heavy the t-shirt weighs or what GSM it is. News flash, nobody cares." `[09:14]`

- **ChatGPT-only research.**
  > "Chad GBT is not going to write you a golf headline about a foresome. I promise you, ChadBt is not going to write that… ChadBt is not going to write you something about spitters and quitters." `[11:29]`
  Reasoning he gives: GPT is downstream of the same averaged corpus as everyone else, so it can't reach niche-specific punchlines.

- **Generic look-alike ads.**
  > "the way that we do that is by not making generic ads that look like everybody else's. It's by doing good research, speaking to the ICP, and testing systematically." `[14:22]`

## 5. Research methodology (action items — would feed → `playbooks/copy-formulas.md` *Research* section)

The verbatim drill:
> "Stop doing tragedy [≈ 'doing it the lazy way']. Spend three or four days in a row just actually doing research on your niche on your product. Go to Reddit. Read your competitor's reviews. Read Amazon reviews. Read comments on Tik Tok videos. Ad comments. Do competitor analysis. Do manual research." `[10:38]`

Output artefact (verbatim):
> "Make a deep research document… not that you're going to paste into Chad GBT, but that you're just going to write stuff. Copy and paste things from Reddit. Copy and paste reviews… on their Amazon… Copy add comments, take screenshots, copy and paste and build a true research document so that you can understand what people are saying." `[12:01]`

**Rule shape (single-source):** Every brief is preceded by a `research/<niche>.md` file containing copy-pasted raw user voice from ≥4 sources (Reddit, competitor reviews, Amazon reviews, TikTok / ad comments). No GPT-summarised research as the only input.

## 6. Brief framework (would feed → `playbooks/copy-formulas.md` *Brief template* section)

Verbatim brief skeleton:
> "the first thing is you need the concept. Then you need the creative type, right? Is it a curiosity based static ad? Us versus them? … USP call out, social proof and review based ad, whatever it is. Always write down your creative type because you don't want to make a hundred of the exact same ad. You do need creative diversity, right? Then you want to call out the angle. What are you actually doing? Who are you actually selling to? Headline, CTA, and visual direction." `[12:39]`
