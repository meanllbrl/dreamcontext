## Net-new delta signals

### §1 — Hook-swap variation strategy (paid-ad-creative)

**Claim:** To overcome ad fatigue without recreating assets from scratch, keep the same core video body (seconds 4–end) and record 5–10 different 3-second hooks. 90%+ of viewers don't make it past the first 3 seconds, so a hook swap is sufficient to reset the "I've seen this" filter.

**Operator formula:**
- Record one base video (e.g. 45 seconds)
- Produce 5–10 hook variants (first 3 seconds each)
- Upload as separate ads with the same body — yields 10 unique ads from 1 shoot
- Apply the same technique when working with influencers: "Record that again with these 5 different opening lines / in these 5 different locations"

**Source:** `_youtube__kuSq-pmNfnM` [05:07–06:02]

**Promotion candidate:** Yes — this is the most concrete creative-production formula in the corpus so far. Should live in `paid-ad-creative` playbook under "creative volume without proportional budget."

### §2 — Turn off by spend, not by ROAS (paid-ad-account-ops → post-launch-optimization)

**Claim:** Within an adset, Meta sometimes uses lower-ROAS ads as top-of-funnel priming for higher-ROAS closers. Killing the low-ROAS ad by ROAS alone breaks the internal funnel Meta has constructed, causing the "winner" to suddenly drop off.

**Operator rule:**
> Turn off ads that Meta has **stopped spending money on**. Do NOT turn off ads solely because they have a lower ROAS than other ads in the same adset.

**Corroboration note:** This is a sharpening of `_youtube__13s-G9Uj51A` §2 ("Don't kill the top-funnel ad"). That video named the pattern; this video adds the explicit decision rule: spend = 0 is the kill trigger, not relative ROAS.

**Source:** `_youtube__kuSq-pmNfnM` [12:31–14:16]

**Promotion candidate:** Yes — merge with `13s-G9Uj51A` §2 when lifting to playbook. The combined rule is: "Within an adset, turn off only when Meta's spend drops to zero; leave low-ROAS ads running if Meta is still allocating budget to them."
